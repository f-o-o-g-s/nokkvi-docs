The Songs view is a flat list of every track in your library. Each row shows the album artwork, title, artist (clickable), album (clickable), duration, and an optional rating / love column.

For navigation, search, multi-select, ratings, and shared right-click actions, see [Library Basics](/guides/library-basics/).

## Sort modes

Songs has the most sort modes of any view:

- **Recently Added** *(default)*, **Recently Played**, **Most Played**, **Favorited**, **Random**
- **Title**, **Album**, **Artist**, **Album Artist**
- **Release Year**, **Duration**, **BPM**, **Channels**, **Genre**, **Rating**, **Comment**

The dynamic column on the right adapts to the current sort — showing year, duration, plays, rating, or genre.

## Columns

Toggle via the gear icon: **Album**, **Genre**, **Stars**, **Plays**, **Duration**, and **Love**. Stars, Plays, and Genre auto-show when you sort by them. Genre stacks under the album when both are visible and takes over the album slot at album-size font when Album is hidden.

## Search

Search hits the Navidrome backend's full-text index and matches across **title, album, artist, and album artist** — typing `radiohead` finds tracks where Radiohead is the artist as well as anything titled "Radiohead".

## Click-through expansion

- **Artist** name on a row → Artists view with that artist expanded inline at the top.
- **Album** name on a row → Albums view with that album expanded inline at the top.
- **Genre** in the dynamic column when sorted by Genre → Genres view with that genre expanded inline.

## Performance with large libraries

Songs loads pages on demand as you scroll. For libraries over ~100k tracks:

- **Random sort** re-samples on every reload, so "Center on Playing" may jump to offset 0 if the playing track isn't in the current sample.
- Filtering through Artist or Album first is usually faster than scrolling Songs.
